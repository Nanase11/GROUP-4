ABACA BAG

An abaca bag is a stylish and eco-friendly accessory made from the fibers of the abaca plant, known for its durability and natural texture. Often handwoven, these bags showcase intricate designs and are perfect for adding a sustainable touch to everyday fashion.

Step-by-Step Process:
Step 1: Harvesting and Preparing the Abaca Fiber
- Harvest mature abaca plants by cutting their stalks close to the ground.
- Strip the fibers from the plant using a stripping knife or similar tool.
- Dry the fibers under the sun until they reach the desired moisture content and are ready for processing.

Step 2: Cleaning and Softening the Fiber
- Wash the dried abaca fibers thoroughly to remove impurities.
- Soften the fibers by pounding or combing them, ensuring they become pliable and easier to weave or process further.

Step 3: Weaving the Fabric
- Use a loom to weave the prepared abaca fibers into a sheet of fabric.
- Choose a plain weave or a more intricate pattern depending on the tote bag's design.
- Ensure the fabric is tightly woven for durability.

Step 4: Cutting the Fabric
- Measure and cut the abaca fabric according to the desired tote bag dimensions. - Cut two identical panels for the front and back and additional strips for handles and side panels, if needed.

Step 5: Sewing the Tote Bag
- Sew the two main panels together along the edges using a strong thread. Leave the top side open.
- If a gusset or side panels are needed, sew these pieces to the front and back panels.
- Fold and stitch the top edges of the bag to create a clean finish.

Step 6: Adding Handles
- Cut two strips of abaca fabric or use pre-made straps.
- Reinforce the handles by layering or braiding the strips for added strength.
- Sew the handles securely to the top edges of the bag. Double-stitch for durability.

Step 7: Reinforcement and Finishing Touches
- Reinforce all seams with additional stitching to ensure the bag can carry heavy items.
- Add decorative elements, such as embroidery, beads, or patches, if desired.

Step 8: Final Inspection
- Inspect the tote bag for loose threads or weak seams.
- Trim excess fabric and threads, ensuring a clean, polished look.

ABACA DRINK COASTER

An abaca drink coaster is a handcrafted, eco-friendly item made from the fibers of the abaca plant. Known for its strength and natural texture, these coasters provide a sustainable way to protect surfaces while adding a rustic, earthy touch to your home or office. The unique weave patterns enhance their aesthetic appeal, making them both functional and decorative.

About

Beyond the Fibers is more than just a company; it is a movement rooted in sustainability, innovation, and community. At its heart, the company values the interconnectedness of nature and human life, using abaca as a symbol of resilience, strength, and renewal. The company believes that every fiber of abaca carries a story — a story of the land it grows in, the hands that cultivate it, and the lives it touches.
